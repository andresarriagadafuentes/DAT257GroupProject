Negatives of Under or Overconsuming Water

Water is an essential nutrient for our bodies, but consuming too little or too much of it can have negative effects. Here are some of the consequences of under or overconsuming water:

Underconsuming Water

Not drinking enough water can lead to:

  • Dehydration
  • Dry mouth and throat
  • Constipation
  • Headaches
  • Fatigue
  • Increased risk of kidney stones

Overconsuming Water

Drinking too much water can lead to:

  • Water intoxication, which can cause confusion, seizures, and even death
  • Electrolyte imbalances
  • Swelling of the brain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Bloating
  • Increased need to urinate

It's important to find a balance and drink enough water to stay hydrated without overdoing it. Generally, it's recommended to drink 8 glasses of water per day, but individual needs may vary depending on factors such as activity level, climate, and overall health.
